Every year in the bitter cold of the Scranton region thousands go unprepared for the coming winter. While basic needs such as food or a roof over their heads are often covered, clothes warm enough to help in the sub-freezing temperature are hard to find and expensive. At Elevate the Electric city we aim to provide new clothing to the disadvantaged of our community, providing them with a sense of both dignity and warmth throughout the winter months.

Why we do it..


From December to February the average temperature in Scranton is below freezing. The cold, paired with blizzards and ice storms can creates an environment that is both deadly and inhospitable. The recent deindustrialization of the North Eastern Pennsylvania region has left many in Scranton unable to afford the costs of heating their homes and warm clothing.

There are many other groups in the region that try to fill the needs of underprivileged Scrantonites, yet we differ in that we try and provide dignity with the clothing we offer. Our clothing drives focus on gathering new and gently used clothing, and our cash donations go to buying new winter clothing out of season in bulk, providing a considerable discount. The clothing we provide allows people to walk to jobs openings in a blizzard, and present as a respectable member of society rather than a vagrant. By providing a respectable image, and raising self-esteem, we can elevate those on the streets to a higher place of being.

Elevate with us!


Elevating the Electric City was founded by local Scrantonites to help revitalize the community by providing a need we have not seen met by our local charities.

Our goal and mission at Elevating the Electric City is to make sure that every resident of Scranton has proper clothing for winter. Through clothing drives, donations from businesses, and cash donations we strive to meet our goals.

The support we have received from our fellow Scranonites and local businesses has amazed us at every turn. Yet despite our growing ability to help the downtrodden of Scranton, every year the demand grows. It is our goal to stay on top of this demand in the upcoming years, and never turn anyone away.

Help us Elevate

Storage Space Needed

In the spring and summer months we easily accumulate stockpiles of winter clothing through various donations and sales. Yet every year we run out of storage space for the clothing we gather, and are forced to rent storage lockers until winter. If you can provide us with space whether it be in your house, place of work, or other property we will be able to spend more money on achieving our goals.

Any space that can be used for clothing drives and distribution is also greatly valued! Warm places where we can distribute our clothing to the less privileged allows them to properly find clothes that fit without being rushed in the middle of a blizzard, and ensures a much smoother process and a larger turn out.


Volunteers

The donations we receive in our clothing drives always astounds us, however we lack the manpower to properly sort and distribute our donations. With more volunteers we will be able reach more people and service more areas.

  • We need volunteers for:
    • Clothing drives
    • Securing donations
    • Maintaining relations with local partners
    • Distributing clothing
    • Inventorying our clothing stocks
    • Mending donations are not in the best of shape

Clothing

Our most pressing need and the core of our mission is acquiring clothing to give to our disadvantaged. Our donations turn up coats, sweaters and jackets in piles, but every year we lack these items.

  • We will not turn away any donation of clothing, but what we desperately need for next winter is:
    • Socks (preferably wool)
    • Hats
    • Gloves
  • Donations of anything else that will help will be gladly accepted. Blankets, portable heaters, cloth, and thread are all incredibly useful in making sure no one in Scranton goes to bed cold.

About Lackawanna College

Situated in the midst of downtown Scranton, Lackawanna College has provided higher education to Lackawanna County region since 1894. Lackawanna College has five satellite campuses, and is in the midst of transitioning from a two year junior college to a full four year school. In spite of the recent increase in student body, Lackawanna College maintains its dedication to it’s students, small class sizes, and personal interaction.

About Phi Beta Lambda

Phi Beta Lambda (PBL) is an organization that pushes students to reach their maximum potential and success in the business world. Various conferences, competitions, and workshops allow students from all over the country to meet and prepare themselves for graduation and the world beyond.

Our website was originally a submission to the Pennsylvania Phi Beta Lambda Website Design competition aiming to demonstrate a working website for a charity organization that provides gently used clothing.

After placing first in the Pennsylvania Phi Beta Lambda Website Design, we have updated our website for the national competition, taking place this year in June at Baltimore.
