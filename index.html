Finding job openings:

The most important part of seeking a new job, is knowing where to look. In the modern age with dozens of different methods for finding jobs, it can be hard to know even where to start.

  • Networking:
    • Do any of your friends or old bosses have openings at their companies? Jobs through someone you know will often give you more information then available in a typical advertisement for help wanted!
  • Career Websites:
    • Websites such as Monster.com provide information on many job openings, and are quick and convenient to use!
  • Job Fairs:
    • Many colleges will host job fairs to help grads or soon to be grads find jobs. Be sure to attend!
  • Internships:
    • Although you may be looking for a fulltime job after graduating, an internship may provide the path to a full time permanent position.

Resumes and Cover Letters

Most often the first chance an employer has to learn anything about you, is through the resume you submit. Having a successful resume will allow you to get your foot in the door, and land the interview.

  • Resume
    • For your resume it is important to include an Objective, Contact information, Education experience, work experience, and any other skills that may be job related.
    • It is important to stand out with your resume, but do not include information that is not pertinent to the job posting. If you are applying for a mechanical engineering job, the resume reader will have no interest in your bass fishing.
    • Make sure the formatting on the resume is consistent, and formal
  • Cover Letter
    • While the resume will merely be a list of facts, the cover letter gives you a chance to be more open ended. In your cover letter be sure to state the position you are applying for, why the company should select you, and possibly give some information on your skills that would fit the job opening.
    • Do not write a long cover letter! Keep it short! Cover letter’s should be no more than a page long.
Completing application forms

Completing a job form is a simple and often short task, but one simple mistake can easily have your resume thrown out

  • Be Neat! - If your application cannot be read, it’ll be thrown out!
  • Read it carefully
    • Make sure you read through it before you begin!
    • Reread the form after finishing it.
  • Be prepared
    • If you are going to be filling out this form in an office, be sure to have all the information required before hand.
    • Information may include:
      • Dates and location of last employment
      • References - Be sure to warn references first of a potential employer calling!
      • Social Security number
Job Interview

If you have had success with all the previously mentioned steps, you may get a call back from the company for a job interview. Making the right impression during an interview can be the most stressful part of a job search, but if you are prepared going in, there is no reason to be afraid.

  • Dress professionally
    • Unless otherwise told, wear a suit(for men) or pantsuit or a nice dress(for ladies)
    • Arrive early!
    • Do not take phone calls or check your phone during the interview
    • Have questions prepared!
    • Expect questions such as:
      • What is your greatest strength? Greatest weakness?
      • What interested you in this opening?
      • Why do you think you would fit with our company?
      • What do you bring to our team?
    • Thank the interviewer.
